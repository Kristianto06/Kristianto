1. Core Business Problem

Despite varying discount budgets and customer acquisition efforts, statistical analysis reveals no significant revenue differences between campaigns.

Statistical Evidence

  • p=0.12 Campaign A vs B: No significant difference
  • p=0.08 Campaign A vs C: No significant difference
  • p=0.06 Campaign B vs C: No significant difference

Business Implications

  • Campaign strategies are not driving measurable revenue growth
  • Questionable ROI on marketing investments
  • Lack of incremental impact from campaign activities

2. Root Cause Analysis

Ineffective Targeting

Campaigns may be too generic, failing to resonate with specific customer segments and incentivize purchases.

Discount Cannibalization

Discounts may primarily attract existing customers who would have purchased anyway, rather than driving incremental sales.

Lack of Differentiation

Campaign mechanics, offers, and themes may be too similar to appeal to different customer needs or stimulate new behaviors.
