Why Feature Design Matters

Before

  • BMI = 28
  • Age = 45

Problem: Model sees these numbers separately. Cannot understand the relationship between BMI and Age.

After

  • BMI_Age = 1,260
  • BMI_squared = 784
  • Age_BMI_Risk = 12.6

Advantage: Model now has new features combining information to capture complex patterns.

Why This Matters:

1. BMI_Age = 1,260 (Interaction Feature)

Concept: Health risk is a combination of BMI and age
Real example:
‚Ä¢ 45 years old with BMI 28 = HIGH RISK ‚ö†Ô∏è
‚Ä¢ 25 years old with BMI 28 = MODERATE RISK ‚ö°
Insight: Model captures that high BMI is more dangerous at older ages

2. BMI_squared = 784 (Non-linear Feature)

Concept: Risk doesn't increase linearly with BMI
Example:
‚Ä¢ BMI 25 ‚Üí 28 (+3 points) = Slight risk increase
‚Ä¢ BMI 28 ‚Üí 31 (+3 points) = MUCH LARGER risk increase (obesity zone)
Insight: BMI¬≤ captures risk acceleration in obesity zones

3. Age_BMI_Risk = 12.6 (Normalized Score)

Concept: Normalized risk score (scale 0-100)
Function: Makes it easier for model to compare risk between individuals
Example:
‚Ä¢ Score 12.6 = Medium-high risk
‚Ä¢ Score 5 = Low risk
‚Ä¢ Score 20+ = Very high risk

Simple Analogy: Car Accident Risk

Before (Raw Data)

‚Ä¢ Speed: 100 km/h
‚Ä¢ Road: Wet

After (Smart Features)

‚Ä¢ Speed √ó Wet_road
‚Ä¢ Speed¬≤ (acceleration risk)

Result: Model knows that 100 km/h on wet road is MUCH more dangerous than 100 km/h on dry road

Key Takeaway:
Combined features help the model understand that a 45-year-old with high BMI is riskier than a 25-year-old with the same BMI. Without feature engineering, the model would give the same prediction for both cases!

Better features, better predictions: accuracy improved from 55% to 65%‚Äîjust by helping the model understand what matters.

Key Takeaways

What we learned from this machine learning project

ML Automates Complex Decisions

What took underwriters 30+ days can now be done in minutes with comparable accuracy, dramatically improving customer experience.

Feature Engineering is Critical

Raw data alone isn't enough. Creating meaningful feature combinations and interactions makes all the difference in model performance.

Ensemble Methods Win

Combining multiple diverse models with smart weighting produces more robust and accurate predictions than any single model.

Ordinal Classification Matters

Risk levels 1-8 have inherent order. Threshold optimization for ordinal targets significantly improves results over simple classification.
