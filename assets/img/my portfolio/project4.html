The project investigates life expectancy in the East Asia & Pacific region in the year 2020. There are significant differences, for example, Papua New Guinea (PNG) with 64.91 years compared to Japan (JPN) with 84.09 years.

    Analysis Results and Insights

    Variation in Life Expectancy:

    Life expectancy in the East Asia and Pacific (EAP) region exhibits considerable variation, though inequality between countries is relatively low. Key metrics include a Standard Deviation (SD) of 6.505, Interquartile Range (IQR) of 10.326, and a Gini Coefficient of 0.0512.

    Model Performance

    • Linear Regression: R² = 1.000 (potential overfitting)
    • Linear GAM: Pseudo R² = 0.99999734
    • Random Forest: R² = 0.9982, RMSE = 0.2570

    Feature Importance

    • life_expectancy_male
    • GDP per capita

    Key Findings by Factor Group:

    Economic Factors

    Strong association between GDP per capita and increased longevity, suggesting investment in healthcare and human capital.

    Healthcare Access

    Per capita health expenditure has increased, while hospital beds and doctors per capita have declined.

    Demographic Factors

    Clear gender disparity with females consistently outliving males by significant margins.

    Education Factors

    Significant positive correlation between education levels and life expectancy across all metrics.

    Recommendations for Papua New Guinea (PNG)

    Papua New Guinea faces significant challenges in improving life expectancy, including limited access to healthcare, low education levels, malnutrition, and environmental factors. Based on our analysis, we recommend the following interventions:

    Healthcare Infrastructure

    • Increase healthcare workers in rural areas
    • Enhance primary and referral services
    • Improve essential medicines availability

    Strategic Partnerships

    • Collaborate with international organizations
    • Engage local governments and communities
    • Develop public-private partnerships

    Implementation Roadmap: Address these areas through a 5-year phased approach focusing first on healthcare infrastructure, followed by education initiatives, and finally economic development programs.
